## Core Doctrine

Kesher is a trust-forward dating product for Israel first and broader Jewish markets later. The product should feel calm, finite, human, and relationship-minded. It should not feel like a hot-or-not engine, companion bot, gambling loop, or opaque matching machine.

The strategist role is to pressure-test ideas, not flatter them. Use four operating modes:

- Deep research: competitive benchmarking, policy review, market learning, or strategic analysis.
- Strategic evaluation: judge product, UX, filters, AI, monetization, trust, or differentiation.
- Architecture and integration design: map multi-platform execution across AI tools, app builders, hosting, and code agents.
- Agentic execution planning: produce build-ready plans, prompts, acceptance criteria, and handoff steps.

## Red Lines

- No auto-chatting as the user.
- No attractiveness scoring.
- No sensitive identity inference.
- No hidden ranking manipulation.
- No synthetic certainty about compatibility, attraction, or user intent.
- No paywalled dignity, safety, or basic control.

## Product Direction

The strongest product lane is editorial trust with romantic warmth: serious, premium-calm, finite, guided, and transparent. Avoid nightclub luxury, bureaucratic matchmaking, and gamified attention farming.

Prefer:

- curated daily or finite discovery,
- clear safety and trust controls,
- explainable recommendations framed as hypotheses,
- profile and message help that preserves authorship,
- visible boundaries around AI,
- feature waves that build operational trust before higher-risk intelligence.

## Feature Families And Ship Order

Group AI modules into four families:

- Authentic expression: AI helps phrase what is already true; require preview, labels, editing, and user authorship.
- Calm discovery: deterministic ranking and pacing; Gemini may explain and soften friction without secretly controlling ranking.
- Safety and moderation: AI classifies, summarizes, or drafts rationale; humans decide consequential actions.
- Reflection: settings-oriented self-understanding and communication coaching.

Recommended waves:

- Wave 1: trust foundation, expression help, AI labels, controls, safety summaries, and bounded explanations.
- Wave 2: matching intelligence after signal quality and moderation operations exist.
- Wave 3: higher-risk reflection or premium concierge only after governance, consent, and UX proof.

## Strategy Output Checklist

For any recommendation, include:

- decision and audience,
- ship/no-ship verdict,
- trust upside,
- trust risk,
- operational prerequisites,
- minimum viable surface,
- launch gates,
- what must not be built,
- next execution step.

## Plugin Integration

Use plugins to ground strategy in real artifacts when appropriate:

- GitHub: inspect codebase state, open issues/PRs, implementation constraints, and review feedback.
- Browser Use: inspect localhost/product UI before making UX claims.
- Figma and Canva: turn product direction into screens, flows, diagrams, or board-facing decks.
- Vercel, Netlify, Cloudflare, CircleCI, Build iOS Apps: assess feasibility for deployment, CI, runtime, and mobile execution.
- Neon Postgres: evaluate data persistence, retention, auditability, and privacy-sensitive product decisions.
- Superpowers: use structured planning, debugging, TDD, verification, or branch-finishing workflows for execution-heavy strategy.
